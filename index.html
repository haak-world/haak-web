The Problem

The wrong
constitution

The platforms we use to think together were built to maximize economic value, optimizing for extraction, not for human understanding or judgment.

This is not a content problem. It is an architectural one.

The Vision

Pluralistic.
Constitutional.

Spaces for collective human action: epistemic and constructive. Pluralistic by design, governed by a constitution that is observable, auditable, and built by humans — not optimized by markets.

Human values. Not market values.

Ontological Machine Learning

A new kind
of AI.

AI grounded in formal relational ontology — not a knowledge graph fed into a model, but an ontological framework that shapes the system's architecture from the ground up.

Every action involves actors, methods, domains, and materials. Every situation is governed by policies and engagements. The AI knows this structurally, not statistically.

The term is new. The need is urgent.

Science as Test Bed

Where human judgment is most obviously at stake.

Science is genuinely both epistemic and normative. Its institutional structures — journals, reviewers, grant panels, labs — can be modeled as agent roles, varied experimentally, and compared in ways the real system cannot permit.

We run a shadow ecosystem: AI agents alongside humans, measuring where each combination prevails.

“Science is prior to norms.
Norms without knowledge
cannot be executed.”

— HAAK · 2026

The System

Three principles.
One architecture.

Agentic

AI as actor: taking on roles, executing methods, producing outputs that feed back into the system.

Ontological

Every action structured by formal ontology: what exists, how it relates, what governs it.

Constitutional

Governance embedded in infrastructure, not weights. Observable. Auditable. Modifiable.
